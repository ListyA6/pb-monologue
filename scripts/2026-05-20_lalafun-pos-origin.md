**Date:** 2026-05-20
**Length:** 45s
**Word count target:** 95–130
**Language:** English VO, English burned captions, Indonesian SRT track

---

## 0. Pre-Brief

- **Topic:** Why I built my own POS for LalaFun.
- **Artifact / proof:** the LalaFun POS dashboard + the old physical "big book" + an Excel file.
- **Open loop:** What did I spend 12 hours a month doing — and what happened when I stopped?
- **Payoff line:** "12 hours a month became 15 minutes. Same restaurant. I just stopped doing the part I hated."
- **Why this fits the brand:** real operator pain → built the thing himself → time delta is the receipt. Pure operator-builder hook.

---

## 1. Hook Variants

```
H1 (number/receipts):    I used to spend 12 hours a month on admin for one restaurant. Now it's 15 minutes.

H6 (fail story):         For 2 years I copied the big book into Excel every Sunday. Pure waste.
H7 (BTS tease):          This is the dashboard that replaced 12 hours of admin a month at LalaFun.
H8 (listicle):           3 admin tasks that used to eat my month — until I built one tool.
H9 (controversial):      Indonesian restaurants don't need more revenue. They need their owners off Excel.
H10 (before/after):      Sunday admin then: big book + Excel + calculator. Sunday admin now: one screen, 4 minutes.
```

**PICKED:** H1 — number/receipts. The 12 hr → 15 min delta IS the entire video. Hook should plant it immediately so the open loop becomes "wait, how?"

**BACKUP:** H10 (before/after) — strong visual hook if we want to A/B with a split-screen open.

## Notes on the choice

The video is structured around ONE delta: 12 hours → 15 minutes. Every B-roll exists to either *establish the 12 hours* (big book, Excel, calculator, error cells) or *prove the 15 minutes* (the POS reveal). Nothing else earns its place in 45 seconds.

The "I built it myself" beat (28–32s) is deliberately short — 8 words. The video isn't about the build, it's about the *delta*. If you want a separate video about *how* you built it (stack, weekend, no team), that's a different script — likely H2 hook lane.
